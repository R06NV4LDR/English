# Conditional Sentences in English Grammar

Conditional sentences describe situations and their possible results. They can be **real** or **unreal**.

## Zero Conditional (Real, General Truths)
**Form:**  
`If + present simple, present simple`

**Examples:**  
- If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.  
- If it rains, the ground gets wet.  

---

## First Conditional (Real Future Possibility)
Used for **real or possible situations in the future**.

**Form:**  
`If + present simple, will + verb`

**Examples:**  
- If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home.  
- If she studies, she will pass the test.  

---

## Second Conditional (Unreal Present/Future)
Used for **imaginary or unlikely situations in the present or future**.

**Form:**  
`If + past simple, would + verb`

**Examples:**  
- If I were rich, I would travel the world.  
- If she studied harder, she would pass the exam.  

---

## Third Conditional (Unreal Past)
Used for **imaginary or impossible situations in the past**.

**Form:**  
`If + past perfect, would have + past participle`

**Examples:**  
- If I had left earlier, I would have caught the train.  
- If they had studied, they would have passed.  

---

## Mixed Conditionals
Used when the **time in the "if" clause** and the **result clause** are different.

**Examples:**  
- If I had studied medicine (past), I would be a doctor now (present).  
- If she were more confident (present), she would have spoken up yesterday (past).  

---

## Key Differences

- **Zero Conditional** → general truths, facts.  
- **First Conditional** → real, possible future events.  
- **Second Conditional** → unreal, imaginary present/future.  
- **Third Conditional** → unreal, imaginary past.  
- **Mixed Conditional** → combination of times.  

## Example sentences

Here are some example sentences for each type of conditional:
#### Zero Conditional

- If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
- If it rains, the ground gets wet.

#### First Conditional

- If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home.
- If she studies, she will pass the test.

#### Mixed Conditional

- If I hadn't overslept, I would be at school on time.

#### Second Conditional

- If the weather were better, I would walk the dogs.. (now/future)

#### Third Conditional

- If the weather had been better, I would have walked the dogs… (past)
- If I hadn’t overslept, I would have been at school on time.
- If the weather had been better, I would have walked the dogs early in the morning.

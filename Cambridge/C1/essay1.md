2. Introduction
When you start living independently as a young adult, a lot of things need to be kept in balance. Having a job absorbs most of the time available and when you get home, the household should get done too. 
The money you've earned will vanish as quickly as you got hold on it. So what can young people learn from the older ones to keep a healthy work-life balance, and to manage how generations before learned to manage?
3. [FIRST KEY POINT]
There are a lot of first times when you grow up: from first taxes to your first real job. And from time to time, things can get a bit overwhelming. It helps to have people with knowledge of these things around you. People who experienced these things before and know how to address them. Although not everyone'll provide the same help, it still makes sense to ask around to find someone who can explain a particular matter you're maybe struggling with.

Do you know how much your tax-bill's gonna be? And how much you need to save, just in case of unexpected costs like a visit at a dentist?

In conclusion, older generations offer a lot of practical knowledge that young people have not yet acquired.
Although advice about work is valuable, guidance on managing money is particularly important, as it directly affects long-term stability and independence.
